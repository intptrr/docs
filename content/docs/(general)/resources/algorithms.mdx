A personal shortlist of the resources I keep coming back to. Quality over
quantity — enough to go from fundamentals to interview-ready and beyond.

<Callout type="info">
  Working through problems? The [LeetCode section](/docs/leetcode) collects
  pattern-based walkthroughs and solutions.
</Callout>

## Books

- **Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS)** — Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein. The
  comprehensive reference; rigorous and exhaustive rather than a beach read.
- **The Algorithm Design Manual** — Steven Skiena. The most practical of the
  bunch, with a "war stories" approach and a useful catalog of problems.
- **Algorithms (4th ed.)** — Sedgewick & Wayne. Approachable, with an excellent
  companion [booksite](https://algs4.cs.princeton.edu/).
- **Grokking Algorithms** — Aditya Bhargava. Illustrated and beginner-friendly;
  the gentlest on-ramp.
- **Competitive Programmer's Handbook** — Antti Laaksonen. Free, concise, and
  great once you're past the basics.

## Practice

- [LeetCode](https://leetcode.com) - The standard for interview-style practice
- [Codeforces](https://codeforces.com) - Competitive programming contests and problems
- [CSES Problem Set](https://cses.fi/problemset/) - A well-structured set covering core techniques
- [AtCoder](https://atcoder.jp) - High-quality contests, strong on math and DP
- [Advent of Code](https://adventofcode.com) - A series of small programming puzzles
- [InterviewDB](https://www.interviewdb.io) - A database of real interview questions and answers

## References & visualization

- [CP-Algorithms](https://cp-algorithms.com) - Clear writeups of algorithms with implementations
- [USACO Guide](https://usaco.guide/) - A comprehensive guide for competitive programming
- [VisuAlgo](https://visualgo.net) - Interactive visualizations of data structures and algorithms
- [OI Wiki](https://oi.wiki) - A wiki for competitive programming
- [Big-O Cheat Sheet](https://www.bigocheatsheet.com) - Quick complexity reference for common operations
