Resources for moving from "I can code" to "I can design systems that don't fall
over." Mix of interview prep and the deeper distributed-systems foundations.

<Callout type="info">
  My own notes and case studies live in the
  [System Design section](/docs/system-design).
</Callout>

## Books

- **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** — Martin Kleppmann. The single best
  starting point; covers storage, replication, partitioning, and consensus with
  rare clarity. ([dataintensive.net](https://dataintensive.net/))
- **System Design Interview, Vol. 1 & 2** — Alex Xu. Interview-focused, with
  worked examples and clear diagrams.
- **Understanding Distributed Systems** — Roberto Vitillo. A concise, modern
  tour of the fundamentals.
- **Database Internals** — Alex Petrov. How storage engines and distributed
  databases actually work under the hood.

## Repositories & guides

- [System Design Primer](https://github.com/donnemartin/system-design-primer) - The canonical open-source study guide; start here
- [ByteByteGo](https://bytebytego.com) - Alex Xu's diagrams, newsletter, and courses

## Blogs & references

- [High Scalability](https://highscalability.com) - Real-world architecture breakdowns of large systems
- [Martin Fowler](https://martinfowler.com) - Patterns and essays on architecture and design
- [AWS Architecture Center](https://aws.amazon.com/architecture/) - Reference architectures and well-architected guidance
- [Google SRE Books](https://sre.google/books/) - Free books on running reliable production systems
- [The Architecture of Open Source Applications](https://aosabook.org/) - How real open-source systems are built

## Lectures & Videos

- [MIT 6.824](http://nil.lcs.mit.edu/6.824/2020/schedule.html) - MIT's course on distributed systems
- [Hello Interview](https://www.youtube.com/@hello_interview) - A YouTube channel with resources about system design

## Papers

- [Papers We Love](https://github.com/papers-we-love/papers-we-love) — Papers from the computer science community
- [MapReduce](https://drive.google.com/file/d/118pdmrWuWsKXgse-ZXjqlu4oEjdR_1bo/view?usp=sharing) — A paper on Google's large-scale data processing model
- [The Google File System](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1k750T6uHGr_FbiT5CWONuO2gyGIaTPQK/view?usp=drive_link) — A paper on Google's distributed file system
- [Raft](https://raft.github.io/) — A paper on a consensus algorithm for managing a replicated log
- Worth reading directly: *MapReduce*, *The Google File System*, *Bigtable*,
  *Dynamo*, and *Spanner*, plus **[Raft](https://raft.github.io/)** for an
  understandable take on consensus.
